Finite Geometric Summation
A geometric series is one that has a common ratio which remains constant between terms.
The sum of a finite geometric series is `a_1(1-r^n)\over(1-r)`, where `a_1` is the first term, `n` is the position of the last term, and `r` is the common ratio. If `r<1` and `n=\infty`, then `r^\infty=0` and `a_1(1-r^\infty)=a_1(1-0)=a_1`, thereby creating the infinite sum equation seen in the previous program.
