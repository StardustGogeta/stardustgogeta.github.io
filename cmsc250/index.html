CMSC250 Handbook

At the University of Maryland at College Park, the CMSC250 Discrete Structures course aggregates much of the mathematical knowledge that is required for progression through the computer science curriculum. In contrast with the conventional meaning of "discrete structures," this course includes topics ranging from probability to mathematical proof to circuit diagrams. In the following pages, I'll try to cover each of these topics in some detail. The goals of this "handbook" are threefold:
  • Provide an introduction to / review of the concepts
  • Give practice problems similar to those on homeworks, exams, etc.
  • Draw connections between topics and concepts
