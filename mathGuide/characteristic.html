Characteristic Equations

One method of solving linear homogeneous differential equations is by finding the corresponding "characteristic equation" for the given situation.
For a differential equation written as `\displaystyle\sum_{k=0}^na_ny^{(n)}=0`, the characteristic equation (a polynomial in `r`) is given by `\displaystyle\sum_{k=0}^na_nr^{n}=0`, for constant coefficients `a_n`.

Each distinct root of the characteristic equation corresponds to a term in the general solution to the differential equation. For roots `\alpha_0`, `\alpha_1`, `\alpha_2`, etc., the general solution is `\displaystyle y=c_0e^{\alpha_0x}+c_1e^{\alpha_1x}+c_2e^{\alpha_2x}+\cdots` for constant coefficients `c_n`.

If there are repeated roots, then there is not an arbitrary coefficient, but rather an arbitrary polynomial in `x`. For instance, with characteristic equation `(r-2)^3=0`, the solution is given by `y=e^{2x}\left(c_0x^2+c_1x+c_2\right)`.
